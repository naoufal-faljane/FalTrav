Georgia Eliminates Visa Requirements for 30+ Countries in 2025 – What Travelers Need to Know

Published on July 15, 2025

In a major move for global tourism, Georgia has announced the elimination of visa requirements for over 30 countries, effective January 2025. The new policy allows visitors from the USA, UK, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, South Korea, and 25 other nations to enter without a visa for up to 365 days.

This landmark decision is expected to significantly boost tourism in Georgia, a country known for its rich history, diverse landscapes, and unique cultural heritage. Travelers can now experience the ancient cities of Tbilisi and Mtskheta, explore the stunning mountain landscapes of Kazbegi, or enjoy the beaches of Batumi without the hassle of visa applications.

Key Changes in the New Policy

The new visa-free regime eliminates the previous 90-day limit within a 180-day period. Now, eligible travelers can stay in Georgia for up to 365 days, opening opportunities for extended stays, remote work arrangements, and deeper cultural experiences.

Visitors will still need to meet certain requirements at the port of entry, including:

  • A valid passport with at least 6 months validity beyond the intended stay
  • Proof of sufficient funds for the duration of the stay (typically $50-100 per day)
  • Proof of onward travel or return journey
  • Travel insurance covering medical expenses

Top Destinations in Georgia

Tbilisi: The capital city offers a unique blend of ancient and modern with its cobblestone streets, sulfur baths, and vibrant arts scene.

Kazbegi: Known for its dramatic mountain scenery and the iconic Gergeti Trinity Church perched on a hill at 2,170 meters.

Batumi: Georgia's Black Sea resort city with beautiful beaches, modern architecture, and a lively atmosphere.

Mtskheta: A UNESCO World Heritage site with ancient churches and historical significance as the former capital.

Tips for Extended Stays

  • Consider the shoulder seasons (April-May and September-October) for fewer crowds and pleasant weather
  • Learn a few Georgian phrases as English is not universally spoken
  • Try traditional dishes like khachapuri and khinkali
  • Plan day trips to wineries in Kakheti region
  • Arrange for travel insurance that covers the entire duration of your stay
