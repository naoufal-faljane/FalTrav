NEOM, Saudi Arabia: The Future City

Published: November 18, 2025 Category: City Guide

Discover NEOM, Saudi Arabia's ambitious megacity project designed to be a model for sustainable urban living. This visionary destination combines cutting-edge technology with sustainable practices to create a city that looks toward the future of human habitation.

About NEOM

NEOM is a planned city in the Tabuk Province of northwestern Saudi Arabia. Announced in 2017 as part of Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030, NEOM aims to be a model of sustainable urban development with no cars, no emissions, and no use of fossil fuels. The project spans 26,500 square kilometers (10,230 square miles) and is expected to be completed in phases, with the first phase of The Line scheduled for completion by 2030. NEOM is positioned as a global destination for innovation, sustainability, and quality of life, attracting investment and talent from around the world.

Key Features of NEOM

  • The Line - A 170-kilometer mirror-structure city designed to house nine million people with zero cars and zero carbon emissions
  • Sustainable Energy - Powered entirely by renewable energy sources including solar and wind
  • Artificial Intelligence Integration - AI will manage infrastructure, transportation, and public services
  • Oxagon - A floating industrial complex shaped like an octagon, designed to be a hub for advanced manufacturing
  • Trojena - A year-round destination in the desert mountains offering wellness, entertainment, and outdoor activities
  • LEENA - A city for AI developers and researchers, designed to foster technological innovation
  • Advanced Transportation - Hyperloop and autonomous systems connecting different zones within NEOM
  • Desert Agriculture - Indoor farming and vertical agriculture to produce sustainable food

Development Phases

  • Phase 1 (2025-2030) - Completion of The Line and initial infrastructure, expected to house 1 million residents
  • Phase 2 (2030-2040) - Expansion of residential and commercial zones, including Trojena and Oxagon
  • Phase 3 (2040+) - Full completion of NEOM's vision, potentially housing 9+ million people
  • Testing Grounds - NEOM serves as a testing environment for new technologies before global deployment
  • Partnerships - International collaborations with tech companies and governments
  • Research Centers - Facilities focused on sustainability, AI, and future technologies

Specialized Zones

  • Sports Arena - Advanced facilities for both traditional and extreme sports
  • Entertainment District - Cutting-edge venues for concerts, shows, and cultural events
  • Research Campus - University and research facilities for innovation and development
  • Wellness & Healthcare - Advanced medical facilities and wellness centers
  • Technology Hub - Centers for AI, robotics, and other high-tech industries
  • Maritime Facilities - Advanced ports and research vessels for Red Sea exploration
  • Energy Systems - State-of-the-art renewable energy production and storage

Environmental Initiatives

NEOM is designed with sustainability at its core:

  • Nature Reserve - Preserving 95% of the natural environment within the region
  • Carbon Negative - The project aims to remove more carbon than it produces
  • Water Security - Advanced water reclamation and desalination technologies
  • Waste Management - Circular economy principles with zero waste to landfill
  • Biodiversity Protection - Conservation programs for local flora and fauna
  • Climate Resilience - Architecture designed to withstand extreme weather
  • Resource Efficiency - Advanced systems for energy, water, and material use

Practical Information for Visitors

  • Language: Arabic is the official language, though English is expected to be widely used
  • Weather: Hot desert climate with extremely hot summers (often exceeding 40°C/104°F) and mild winters
  • Time Zone: AST (GMT+3)
  • Currency: Saudi Riyal (SAR), though NEOM may introduce digital currencies
  • Transportation: Autonomous vehicles, hyperloop, and other futuristic transportation systems
  • Visa Requirements: Expected to have unique visa policies, possibly including digital nomad visas
  • Technology Requirements: High-speed connectivity and digital infrastructure integrated throughout
  • Climate Considerations: Air conditioning systems and climate-controlled outdoor spaces

Getting to NEOM

NEOM will feature its own international airport and seaport. The city is located near the Jordan and Egyptian borders on the Red Sea coast. International visitors will be able to reach NEOM via:

  • New NEOM International Airport (planned)
  • King Abdulaziz International Airport in Jeddah with high-speed rail connection
  • Potentially via sea transport from other Red Sea ports
  • Hyperloop connections to major regional cities
  • Private yacht marinas for luxury travelers

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit NEOM will be during the cooler months (October to April) when temperatures are more comfortable. Summer months (May to September) are extremely hot and will likely have limited outdoor activities. The city's climate-controlled infrastructure will make it accessible year-round, but outdoor exploration will be more pleasant during cooler months.

Travel Requirements

As a new destination, NEOM will likely have specific entry requirements:

  • Valid passport for international visitors
  • Special NEOM visa (may differ from standard Saudi visa)
  • Travel insurance covering medical care in the destination
  • Digital health credentials for entry to certain facilities
  • Agreement to comply with NEOM's technology and data policies
  • Background checks for security purposes
  • Proof of financial means for extended stays

NEOM represents a bold vision for the future of urban living, combining cutting-edge technology with sustainable practices. As this ambitious project develops, it promises to become a unique destination for those interested in innovation, sustainability, and the future of human habitation.
